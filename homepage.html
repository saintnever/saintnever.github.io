Biography

Tengxiang Zhang is an assistant researcher at Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, where he works at the Pervasive Computing Research Center on ultra low-power sensing and distributed interactive systems for Human-centered Computing. Dr. Zhang's research focuses on (1) ss, (2) bb, and (3) cc. He graduated with honor in Computer Science and Technology from Tsinghua University in 2019, where he focused on human computer interaction and was advised by Prof. Yuanchun Shi. He has 10+ publications in top-tier conferences and journals including CHI and IMWUT. He received his M.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Texas at Austin in 2013, where he focused on electromagnetics and was advised by Prof. Andrea Alu. He received his B.S. from Chien-Shiung Wu Honors College, Southeast University in 2011.

Research Interests

My research lines in the technical side of human computer interaction and ubiquitous computing. I adopt a human-centered perspective to enable ubiquitous sensing and distributed interaction in smart spaces. I establish both communication and power links between on-body devices (e.g. smart watches, smart phones) to off-body everyday objects for interaction and sensing purposes. I do so by developing novel sensing hardware and algorithms, and interaction techniques. Specifically, I’m developing tags and wearable input devices that when combined can provide a more spontaneous and consistent interaction experience. The coming of the AI + IoT era is merging smart devices and services into our daily lives, benefitting numerous industries along the way. As technological advances continue to blur the line between the digital media and the physical world, there is a constant demand for effective sensing approaches as well as natural interactive interfaces. My work focuses on building sensing and interactive systems using my expertise in HCI, sensing, embedded systems, signal processing, and machine learning.

The application-driven research object is by nature interdisciplinary. Hence, I collaborate not only with experts in computer science and electrical engineering but also across design-thinking, business as well as industries. Through these close collaborations, I am able to build high-impact, novel pervasive systems that incorporate cutting-edge research towards impactful applications such as health, wellness and accessibility.
