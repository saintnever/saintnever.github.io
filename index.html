Biography

Tengxiang Zhang is an assistant researcher at Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, where he works at the Pervasive Computing Research Center. Dr. Zhang graduated with honor in Computer Science and Technology from Tsinghua University in 2019, where he focused on human computer interaction and was advised by Prof. Yuanchun Shi. He has 10+ publications at top venues including SIGCHI and IMWUT. He received his M.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Texas at Austin in 2013, where he focused on electromagnetics and was advised by Prof. Andrea Alu. He received his B.S. from Chien-Shiung Wu Honors College, Southeast University in 2011, where he worked as an undergraduate research assistant in Prof. Tiejun Cui's lab.

Research Interests

My research lines in the technical side of human computer interaction and ubiquitous computing. I adopt a human-centered perspective to enable distributed interaction and ubiquitous sensing in smart spaces. My methodology is to establish both communication and power links between on-body devices and off-body everyday objects. Then leverage and redistribute the computation and energy resources of today's powerful on-body devices (e.g. smart phone, smart watch) to make off-body objects 'smart' for an effortless pervasive interaction experience. Specifically, I 1) build novel wearable input/output devices, 2) augment everyday objects with ultra-low-power backscatter tags, 3) design distributed interaction interfaces, and 4) develop context sensing techniques. I'm also interested in applying the above concepts, devices, and algorithms to solve building-scale and city-scale problems.
